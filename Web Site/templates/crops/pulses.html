Information about Pulses

Pulses are a group of leguminous crops rich in proteins and essential nutrients. They include chickpeas, lentils, peas, and kidney beans, which are widely grown for human consumption. Pulses thrive in well-drained loamy soil with moderate rainfall and require minimal irrigation. These crops fix nitrogen in the soil, improving soil fertility.

Best Harvesting Techniques

Pulses are harvested when the pods dry out and turn brown, indicating seed maturity. Manual harvesting involves cutting plants and allowing them to dry before threshing. Mechanical harvesters are used in large-scale farms for efficiency. Post-harvest drying is essential to reduce moisture content below 12% for safe storage. Proper storage in cool, dry conditions prevents pest infestations.
