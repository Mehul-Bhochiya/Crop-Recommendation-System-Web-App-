The suggested crop is Millets

Information about Millets

Millets are small-seeded grains known for their high nutritional value and drought resistance. Common varieties include pearl millet, finger millet, and sorghum. They grow well in dry, sandy soil with minimal water requirements, making them ideal for arid and semi-arid regions. Millets are rich in fiber, iron, and protein, making them a staple in many diets.

Best Harvesting Techniques

Millets are harvested when the grains harden and turn golden brown. Manual harvesting is common, where farmers cut the stalks and dry them before threshing. In large farms, combine harvesters are used for efficiency. After threshing, millet grains should be dried to reduce moisture below 12% for safe storage. Proper storage in airtight containers prevents spoilage and pest infestation.
