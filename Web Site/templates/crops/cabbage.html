Information about Cabbage

Cabbage is a leafy vegetable rich in fiber and essential nutrients. It grows well in cool climates with moist, well-drained soil. The plant forms a compact head of leaves that are harvested at maturity. Cabbage is widely used in salads, stir-fries, and fermented foods.

Best Harvesting Techniques

Cabbage is harvested when the heads are firm and reach the desired size. Manual harvesting involves cutting the heads with a sharp knife, while mechanical harvesters speed up the process in commercial farming. Proper handling ensures freshness, and harvested cabbages should be stored in a cool, humid place to prevent wilting.
