The suggested crop is Mustard

Information about Mustard

Mustard is an oilseed crop grown for its seeds, which are used for oil extraction and spice production. It grows well in cool climates with well-drained loamy soil and moderate moisture. The mustard plant produces small yellow flowers, followed by seed pods. Mustard oil is widely used for cooking and medicinal purposes.

Best Harvesting Techniques

Mustard is harvested when the seed pods turn yellow-brown and become dry. Manual harvesting involves cutting the plants and drying them in bundles before threshing. In large-scale farming, combine harvesters are used for efficient collection. The seeds should be dried to a moisture level of 8-10% before storage to prevent fungal growth. Proper storage in airtight containers ensures long-term usability.
