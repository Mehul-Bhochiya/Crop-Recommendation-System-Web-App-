Information about Sugarcane

Sugarcane is a tropical and subtropical crop grown primarily for sugar production. It is a tall, perennial grass that requires warm temperatures, abundant sunlight, and well-drained loamy soil. It has a long growing cycle of 10-18 months, depending on the variety and climatic conditions. Sugarcane is widely used in sugar mills, ethanol production, and the beverage industry.

Best Harvesting Techniques

Sugarcane is harvested when the stalks reach full maturity, typically when the lower leaves start drying, and the juice content is highest. Manual harvesting involves cutting stalks close to the ground using machetes, while mechanized harvesters are used for large-scale farms. Harvesting should be done efficiently to minimize sucrose loss. Once harvested, sugarcane should be transported quickly to processing units to prevent juice deterioration.
